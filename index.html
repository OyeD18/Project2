YAP - Roommate Connection App

A mobile app designed by university students, for university students to enhance communication and strengthen relationships through interactive, gamified experiences.

Role

UI/UX Designer – I contributed to interface design, usability testing, and the refinement of prototypes. My focus was on translating low- and mid-fidelity wireframes into high-fidelity designs while ensuring smooth and intuitive navigation.

Scope

8 Weeks, October 2, 2024 to November 27, 2024.

Tools

Figma, Adobe Illustrate

Background

My group and I were tasked with finding a unique porblem, and then producing a service or product that would solve this problem for the users.
The core problem we aimed to solve was ineffective communication leading to misunderstandings and conflicts. Initially, our focus was on roommates struggling to get along. However, we quickly realized that the need for better communication extended beyond just shared living situations.
This led to the development of an interactive app with fun, game-based features designed to help people understand each other better and build meaningful connections. The app aims to break down first impressions, encourage deeper conversations, and promote open-minded interactions.

Design Process

Our early ideas revolved around a general roommate-planning app that included task management and organizational features. However, we noticed our focus was too broad, resulting in disconnected features that failed to address the root causes of roommate conflicts—poor communication and lack of understanding.
After reevaluating our approach, we shifted our focus toward fostering connection and meaningful interactions. This shift transformed our app into a platform centered on improving communication rather than just managing roommate logistics.

Core Features

  1. Interactive "Getting to Know You" Games
    2.

    Users can play mini-games that reveal personal insights, interests, and experiences. This creates a playful, pressure-free environment for users to bond and build rapport.

  2. Daily Connection Prompts
    3.

    Users receive a prompt encouraging conversation on thought-provoking topics or shared experiences daily. This habit-forming interaction helps strengthen relationships over time.

  3. Shared Memories & Goals Tracker
    4.

    Users can set and track goals or events such as cooking together, fitness challenges, or hobby exploration. Celebrating small wins together helps build a sense of teamwork and collaboration.

Prototyping & Design Insights

We began with low-fidelity prototypes, focusing on structure and functionality.

  • Early designs focused too much on the interface rather than experience.
  • Competitive research (e.g., Lumosity) helped refine gamified elements.
  • Specificity in prototyping was essential for conveying unique features effectively.

Problems & Solutions

Problem 1: The search filter in the games section lacked depth | Solution: Introduced topic-based filtering to help users find relevant discussions.

Problem 2: The app was limited to only friends that you knew | Solution: Added a feature allowing users to connect with new people who share similar experiences.

Takeways

  • Designing for connection requires moving beyond functionality to creating engaging, meaningful experiences.
  • User insights are crucial, implimenting changes based on feedback ensures relevance and usability.
